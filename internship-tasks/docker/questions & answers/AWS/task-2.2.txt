1. What makes a subnet public?
A subnet is public when its route table has a route to the Internet Gateway (0.0.0.0/0 → IGW).
This allows resources in that subnet to access the internet directly.

2. What makes a subnet private?
A subnet is private when it does not have a direct route to the Internet Gateway.
It uses a NAT Gateway for internet access instead.

3. Why do we use a NAT Gateway?
We use a NAT Gateway to allow private subnet resources to access the internet safely, 
without exposing them to incoming traffic.

4. Can a private subnet receive traffic directly from the internet?
No. A private subnet cannot receive direct traffic from the internet.
It only allows outbound traffic through the NAT Gateway.

5. What happens if a private subnet is connected to an Internet Gateway?
It becomes a public subnet and its resources will be exposed to the internet, which is not secure.

6. Why is this architecture production-ready?
Because it keeps public and private resources separate.
Public services are exposed, but sensitive services stay protected in private subnets.
